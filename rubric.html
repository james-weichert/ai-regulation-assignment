AI Regulation Assignment

SIGCSE 2026 Nifty Assignment Submission

Learning Objectives

  1. Recognize limitations in the alignment of large language models, and describe how and why these limitations can be used to produce undesired outputs.
  2. Apply understanding of AI alignment limitations to jailbreak and then subsequently align an open source LLM.
  3. Evaluate the success of technical alignment interventions, and consider the role of policy and governance structures in the responsible development and deployment of AI.

Assignment Grading Rubric

Criteria Full Credit Partial Credit No Credit
Understanding LLM Limitations (LO1)

Descriptions of why and how LLM alignment mechanisms can be circumvented are provided.

 Describes a key limitation of the LLM (e.g. inability to censor itself, sycophancy, etc.) and how this limitation enables a jailbreak. Identifies a limitation of the LLM, but does not sufficiently explain how this limitation enables a jailbreak. Does not explicitly identify a limitation of the LLM.
Scope and Implementation (LO2)

Scope of jailbreak and alignment is appropriate, and implementation is well-documented.

 Describes the steps taken to jailbreak and align the chosen model, elaborating on any challenges or successes encountered. Effort is made to jailbreak or align the model, but the process is not elaborated upon. Model jailbreak or alignment is not attempted.
Writeup (LO3)

Difficulty and robustness of jailbreaking and alignment attempts are evaluated.

 Discusses the robustness of the jailbreaking and alignment solutions, and describes the ease of implementing the solutions. Discusses the robustness of the jailbreaking and alignment solutions, or describes the ease of implementing the solutions, but not both. Neither robustness nor ease of implementation are discussed.
Policy Considerations (LO3)

Demonstrated engagement with AI policy implications.

 Proposes a policy to avoid the relevant harm and evaluates the feasibility of implementing this policy in code. A policy is proposed, but its feasibility is not evaluated. A policy is not proposed.

Notes

  • The emphasis of this assignment is intentionally placed on the students' engagement with the broader social, ethical, and policy implications of AI alignment. As such, most rubric items are oriented to assess this engagement, and technical efficacy and robustness of students' jailbreaking and alignment attempts are de-emphasized. However, the rubric could be modified to more closely assess technical achievements in the context of, for example, a natural language processing course where students should be proficient in using and adapting LLMs.
  • The rubric currently combines the jailbreaking and alignment parts of the assignment, but these could be separated into separate parts of the rubric, each with their own "scope", "writeup", and "policy considerations" criteria.
